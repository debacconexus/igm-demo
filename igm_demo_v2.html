How IGM Works
For Non-Technical Users
When an AI system receives a question, it normally generates as many words as it wants — often hundreds or thousands. The IGM intercepts that request before the AI starts generating, sets a limit on how much it can say, and ensures the answer is structured, bounded, and appropriate for the situation.

Think of it like a triage nurse who decides what level of care a patient needs before sending them to a doctor — not after.
Architecture Modules
GATE
Validates the request and detects language, domain, and complexity before anything else runs.
NERP
Sets the token budget — the maximum the AI can say. Enforced before the model runs, not after.
ZITG
Zero-Inference Trust Governance. Verifies model integrity. Ensures no ungoverned states exist.
LLM
The AI model runs — but only within the constraints set by GATE + NERP. It cannot exceed the governed ceiling.
OutputGateway
Signs every governed response with a cryptographic key. Creates an immutable, verifiable record — like a notary stamp on the output.
What the Savings Mean
🟢Tokens saved — fewer words generated = less computing power used
⚡Energy (mWh) — measurable reduction in electricity consumed by data center servers
💧Water (mL) — data centers use water for cooling; fewer tokens = less cooling required
🌿CO₂ (mg) — proportional to energy reduction; governance reduces carbon emissions
💰Cost saved — direct compute cost reduction based on token differential
The Cryptographic Signature
Every governed inference is signed with an Ed25519 cryptographic key and assigned a unique catalogue ID. This creates an immutable, verifiable record that the output was governed — who governed it, when, under what constraints. This is the audit trail required for SB-53 compliance and public agency accountability.
